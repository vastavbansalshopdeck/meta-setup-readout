Executive summary · Onboarding Ops · Pilot 7–8 July 2026

Problem & Hypothesis

Problem. Meta (Facebook) setup is a mandatory onboarding step before a seller can receive orders. Today it runs on POC-scheduled 1:1 calls — slow to book, easy to delay, and a direct drag on the onboarding timeline. Scheduling was constrained by POC availability, not seller need.

Hypothesis. An on-demand live-connect lobby — seller taps a link when ready, lands in a queue, gets routed to the next free POC — removes scheduling friction, shortens calls (sellers arrive ready), and lets a smaller team clear more setups per day.

What we knew before the pilot

Recorded call data (4 days, no live activity)
  • Median call 75 min; concurrency peaked ~11–13.
  • The apparent 3–6 PM "peak" was a supply artifact — POCs only worked 10–7, so demand got pushed there.
  • ~22% scheduled calls were no-shows.
Two seller surveys (n=34 done, n=117 upcoming)
  • 43% want "instant connect" over booking — validates the lobby model.
  • True demand is a broad afternoon plateau, not a spike; 19% want evening 7–10 PM (invisible in logs).
  • 21% are weekend-only — unreachable on a Mon–Sat week.

What we did — 2-day live pilot

Seller taps link → enters lobby → coordinator routes → POC breakout room → setup + logged

One Google Meet with a breakout room per POC; a human coordinator in the main room assigned each waiting seller to a free POC. Ran 7 July (4 POCs early → 10 later) and 8 July (~9–10 sustained).

What worked
  • Handle time fell ~28% (75→54 min) — sellers arrive ready.
  • ~4.8–5.8 completed setups per effective POC (time-weighted), at only 66% occupancy → real slack.
  • Peak concurrency 12 — matches our design ceiling.
What broke
  • 5-min lobby SLA missed — median wait 6 min, 34% waited >10 min. Cause: manual coordinator serialises routing.
  • Failures are structural & seller-side — FB/2FA restrictions, no laptop — knowable before the call.

Next action — productise

The two failure modes are process problems, not headcount problems. Fixing them in-product is the next step:

Staffing case — Meta + OB task completion

Targets: 50–60 Meta setups/day and 60–70 sellers/day to OB-task completion. Non-Meta OB bundle per seller = webd 0.5 + DTR 0.5 + PG 0.25 + fund 0.25 + calls 0.25 = 1.75 h (worst) / 1.25 h best (automated webd & DTR). Meta productivity: 6/POC best, 4.5 worst. +15% buffer on top.

Bandwidth saving. Against today's 45 staffed, the model needs ~25 people (best) to ~34 (worst) — freeing 11–22 heads (≈24–49%) to redeploy across the funnel or absorb growth. Midpoint (55 Meta + 65 OB/day): ~25–34 people.

Basis: 4-day recorded call logs + 2 seller surveys (n=34, n=117) + 2-day live pilot (7–8 July, 129 sellers). Pilot figures are directional (2 days, some logging gaps). Effective-POC = time-weighted for staggered staffing. Staffing assumes ~6.5 productive hrs/person/day; buffer 15% for leave/attrition.
