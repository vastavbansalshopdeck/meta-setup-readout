Meta Setup: Live-Connect Pilot

Executive summary · Onboarding Ops · Pilot 7–8 July 2026
← Index · Full readout · Appendix

Problem & Hypothesis

Problem — Meta setup is mandatory before orders flow, but POC-scheduled calls run long and leave POCs underutilised — a costly bottleneck on onboarding throughput.

Hypothesis — An on-demand lobby routing sellers to the next free POC removes friction and lets a smaller team clear more setups.

What we knew before the pilot

  • The old 3–6 PM call "peak" was a supply artifact (POC hours), not real seller demand.
  • Surveys (n=34, n=117): 43% want instant connect.
  • Evening (19%) and weekend-only (21%) sellers were going unserved entirely.

What we did — & what worked

Two-day live pilot: seller taps a link → lobby → coordinator routes to a POC breakout room. 10–13 POCs, 129 sellers served.

Efficiency gains & staffing

Targets: 50–60 Meta setups/day + 60–70 sellers/day to OB-task completion (OB bundle fixed at 1.75h/seller). Only Meta productivity varies: 6/POC best, 4.5 worst. +15% buffer.

Next action

Productise the lobby for a projected ~20–25% throughput lift on the same headcount: auto-route sellers to the first free POC, killing the coordinator bottleneck and hitting the <5-min wait SLA.

Roll out on two overlapping shifts (early + evening) on weekdays, with a single small shift on weekends.
